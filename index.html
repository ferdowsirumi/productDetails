Issues and Strategies

Protected Area systems are both ENDS (protected areas directly conserve genetic resources, species, ecosystems and ecosystem processes) and MEANS (to enable many other thematic conservation objectives through in situ implementation, governance and equitable sharing). IUCN's Global Protected Areas Programme (GPAP) has three priority areas as outlined below.

Valuing and conserving biodiversity

GPAP focuses on meansuring progress of attainment of biodiversity conservation targets in collaboration with national goverments and global standards.

Governing nature’s use and sharing its benefits equitably

This strategy focuses on involving local people and government as well as linking soverign states. Secondly, the program focuses on fair distribution of rights, resources and cost among involved parties.

Deploying nature-based solutions to global challenges

GPAP prioratizes on well managed, connected solutions for conservation of socio-economic systems involving a Portected Area. GPAP facilitate development of resources, train conservation professionals and develop standards for the attainment of the Protected Areas.
